THE WESTGATE ATTACK

THE 80-HOUR TERRORIST ATTACK IN NAIROBI, KENYA, TOOK PLACE BETWEEN THE 21ST AND 24TH SEPTEMBER 2013, RESULTING IN 67 DEATHS AND OVER 175 WOUNDED

THE SOMALI-BASED ISLAMIC TERRORIST GROUP AL-SHABAAB CLAIMED RESPONSIBILITY FOR THE ATTACK

THE UPMARKET WESTGATE SHOPPING MALL WAS A WELL-KNOWN CENTRE OF WESTERN EXPATRIATE LIFE IN NAIROBI. PREVIOUS WARNINGS SUGGESTED THE COMPLEX WAS IN DANGER OF A TERROR ATTACK.

the attack took place on a busy Saturday morning, with approximately 1,000 people inside the popular mall. A local radio station was hosting a children's cooking competition on the roof of the building. As the attack began, the winners were about to receive their prizes.

at least 67 people are known to have died, including children and four attackers. over 175 were injured.

The picture that has emerged in the aftermath of the attack is that of a woefully disorganised and incompetent response from Kenyan authorities. a clash of egos left a handful of Kenyan officers and armed vigilantes to initially fight heavily armed militants, in a bid to rescue hundreds of shoppers.

Twitter was used a main communication channel BY all participants: the terrorist group, the victims, government, ngos, and the mass media. Major news websites, TV channels and newspapers around the world actually based their reports on the event tweets, thus creating an arguably unprecedented pattern of mass media information collection.
